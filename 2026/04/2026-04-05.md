1. ⭐ LinkS²Bench: UAV-Satellite Dynamic Cross-View Spatial Intelligence `arXiv:2604.02020`

   - Why it matters: Directly exposes capability gaps in RS foundation models — multi-sensor cross-view reasoning is unsolved.

2. SPAR: Single-Pass Any-Resolution ViT for Open-Vocabulary Segmentation `arXiv:2604.02252`
   - Distills sliding-window ViT capability into a single-pass model via feature regression; achieves up to +10.5 mIoU over single-pass baselines.
   - No extra parameters or architectural changes — efficient high-resolution open-vocabulary segmentation.
   - Why it matters: Scalable open-vocab segmentation applicable to large-scale RS land cover mapping.

🤖 AI & Foundation Models

3. CliffSearch: Agentic Co-Evolution over Theory and Code for Scientific Algorithm Discovery `arXiv:2604.01210`
   - LLM agents act as evolutionary operators (crossover, mutation, review) over theory+code artifact pairs to autonomously discover novel algorithms; validated on transformer optimizer discovery.
   - Reviewer-gate filters ensure correctness and originality at each evolution step.
   - Why it matters: Structured agentic evolution + peer-review can fully automate scientific algorithm search.

4. Omni-SimpleMem: Autoresearch-Guided Discovery of Lifelong Multimodal Memory `arXiv:2604.01007`
   - Fully autonomous research pipeline runs ~50 experiments without human input to discover a unified multimodal memory framework; F1 +411% on LoCoMo benchmark.
   - Demonstrates a self-improving AI loop that designs and validates its own architecture improvements.
   - Why it matters: Milestone for the "AI scientist" — first end-to-end autonomous AI research at this scale.

5. OrgAgent: Organize Your Multi-Agent System like a Company `arXiv:2604.01020`
   - Hierarchical MAS with governance/execution/compliance layers inspired by corporate structures; 102% performance gain and 74% token reduction vs. flat multi-agent baselines.
   - Separates strategic planning from task execution to enable scalable, low-overhead agent coordination.
   - Why it matters: Practical blueprint for efficient large-scale multi-agent scientific pipelines.

6. Multi-Agent Human-LLM Collaborative Framework for Closed-Loop Scientific Literature Summarization `arXiv:2604.01452`
   - Elhuyar combines LLMs and human scientists for iterative closed-loop literature review; uncovers hidden correlations (He bubble growth vs. dose/temperature) missed by conventional search.
   - Structured agent-human collaboration catches overlooked findings and fills literature gaps.
   - Why it matters: Practical template for AI-assisted systematic literature review in Earth science and RS.
