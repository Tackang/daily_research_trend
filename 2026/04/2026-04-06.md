🔬 Remote Sensing & Earth Observation

1. ⭐ Prior2DSM: Test-Time Adaptation for Height Completion via Self-Supervised ViT and Monocular Foundation Models `arXiv:2604.02009`
   - Training-free DSM completion combining self-supervised ViT features with monocular depth foundation models; converts relative depth to metric heights without retraining. Achieves up to 46% RMSE reduction vs. linear fitting on satellite/aerial imagery.
   - Why it matters: Efficient path to adapt general-purpose foundation models to RS height estimation with zero retraining overhead.

2. YieldSAT: A Multimodal Benchmark for High-Resolution Crop Yield Prediction `arXiv:2604.00940`
   - Multi-country dataset (4 countries, 4 crops, 12.2M yield samples) paired with 10m Sentinel-2 time series; domain-informed Deep Ensemble improves pixel-level crop yield prediction across diverse geographies.
   - Why it matters: Fills a critical benchmark gap for pretraining RS foundation models on large-scale agricultural satellite time series.

3. ProVG: Progressive Visual Grounding via Language Decoupling for Remote Sensing `arXiv:2604.01893`
   - Decomposes natural language queries into global context, spatial relations, and object attributes to localize objects in satellite imagery via a survey-locate-verify scheme with cross-scale fusion. SOTA on RRSIS-D and RISBench.
   - Why it matters: Language-guided grounding in RS imagery is a key capability for interactive RS foundation model interfaces.

4. PC-SAM: Patch-Constrained Interactive Road Segmentation in High-Resolution RS Images `arXiv:2604.00495`
   - Constrains SAM point prompts to specific patches for fine-grained road extraction in satellite images; outperforms automatic SOTA methods while adding flexible interactive refinement.
   - Why it matters: Lightweight SAM adaptation strategy for RS that bridges domain gap without full retraining.

🤖 AI & Foundation Models

5. GeoAI Agency Primitives `arXiv:2604.01869`
   - Proposes 9 agency primitives (navigation, perception, geo-referenced memory, dual modeling, etc.) as a conceptual vocabulary bridging geospatial foundation models with practical GIS workflows.
   - Why it matters: Provides a structured framework for building RS-specific AI agents on top of foundation models—directly actionable for EO deployment.

6. A Visionary Look at Vibe Researching `arXiv:2604.00945`
   - Introduces "vibe researching"—a human-AI collaborative research mode where LLM multi-agent systems handle literature review, experimentation, and manuscript preparation under human direction. Identifies 7 key technical challenges.
   - Why it matters: Concrete near-future vision for how AI agents will reshape the scientific research pipeline.

7. EvoSkills: Self-Evolving Agent Skills via Co-Evolutionary Verification `arXiv:2604.01687`
   - Couples an iterative Skill Generator with a co-evolving Surrogate Verifier to autonomously develop and refine multi-step agent skills without manual engineering. Strong results on SkillsBench across multiple LLMs.
   - Why it matters: Reduces manual effort for deploying capable AI agents in specialized scientific domains like Earth observation.

8. BloClaw: An Omniscient Multi-Modal Agentic Workspace for Scientific Discovery `arXiv:2604.00550`
   - Replaces fragile JSON protocols with XML-Regex Dual-Track Routing (0.2% vs. 17.6% error rate), supports dynamic visualization capture; evaluated on cheminformatics, protein folding, and molecular docking.
   - Why it matters: Robust agentic orchestration infrastructure is a prerequisite for reliable AI-accelerated science at scale.

💡 One-line comment: The RS-AI frontier is shifting from perception to agency—foundation models are increasingly embedded in agentic loops that actively query, verify, and evolve domain knowledge.
